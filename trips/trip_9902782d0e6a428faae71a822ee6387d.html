My Morning Walk - April 11, 2025

The walk began on a crisp spring morning around 10:00 AM. The sun was shining, and the sky was a brilliant blue with only a few clouds.

I started somewhere near the National Research Council Canada headquarters. Did you know that the NRC is the Government of Canada's premier research and development organization?

After about five minutes, I found myself near the Rideau River. This river is part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site Rideau Canal. It flows north through Ottawa and empties into the Ottawa River.

By 10:08 I was nearing the **Rideau Tennis Club** . Lawn tennis was introduced to Canada in 1876 and it was played extensively at Rideau Hall.

A bit later, around 10:15, I'd continued south and walked past Strathcona Park. Strathcona Park is named after Lord Strathcona, who helped finance the Canadian Pacific Railway.

At approximately 10:20, I was east of the park, in a more residential area. I turned around shortly after.

My last location was reached around 10:30, which was back near the park. It seemed like the pace slowed down, after Strathcona Park. All in all it was a pleasant walk, that I will definitely be repeating!

A Stroll Through Ottawa

Today's walk, a little over 2 kilometers and taking about 45 minutes, was a pleasant exploration of downtown Ottawa. I started near the Supreme Court of Canada. It's interesting to know that its construction was delayed by World War II and the building was only finally completed in 1940!

Heading southeast, I soon found myself near Library and Archives Canada. The library's collection even includes the original manuscript of Anne of Green Gables!

My journey continued, taking me closer to Parliament Hill. Did you know the Centre Block's Peace Tower contains a memorial chamber dedicated to Canadians who have given their lives in service?

After that, I was near the Ottawa River shoreline. Did you know that the Ottawa River was a crucial transportation route for the early fur trade?

Turning back slightly, I passed near the National Arts Centre (NAC). The NAC is bilingual, with programming in both English and French!

Finally, I looped back, again passing near the NAC and the Rideau Canal, a UNESCO World Heritage site that transforms into the world's largest skating rink in the winter!

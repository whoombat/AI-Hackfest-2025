A Stroll Through Ottawa

Date: October 26, 2023 (Imaginary)

Time: Afternoon

Started my walk near the Supreme Court of Canada. What a grand building! Did you know that only judges can wear robes in the Supreme Court? The rest of us must be on our best behavior.

Took a relaxing meander eastwards, passing along close to the Ottawa River. This river served as a crucial transportation route for the early logging industry. It's hard to imagine it bustling with log booms and voyageurs today!

Cut through a small neighborhood until I was closer to the Garden of the Provinces and Territories. The Garden has a plaque from each province and territory that represent the identity and history of those regions. This spot is particularly lovely in the fall when the trees are changing colors.

The route took me further south towards Confederation Park, a sprawling green space in the heart of the city. It is known for hosting festivals. The park is home to several monuments, with lots of people sitting and relaxing in the sun.

Ventured eastward again, eventually making my way near Rideau Canal, though not right alongside it. The canal transforms into the world's largest skating rink in winter! What a transformation.

Finally, the stroll took me back in a northwesterly direction and ended. In total, the walk was approximately 2 kilometers and took about 30-45 minutes at a leisurely pace. A pleasant way to spend an afternoon!

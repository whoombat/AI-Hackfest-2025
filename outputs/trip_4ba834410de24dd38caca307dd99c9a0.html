April 12, 2025

The city exhaled, a gentle breeze whispering through downtown Ottawa. From the starting point the walk began at around 2 PM, near the vibrant ByWard Market. This historic hub is one of Canada's oldest markets, where you can find everything from local produce to unique crafts.

The path then gently curved, leading westwards, towards Parliament Hill. Its iconic Peace Tower stands tall, a symbol of Canada's democratic values. Did you know its construction was completed in 1927, replacing the Victoria Tower that was destroyed by fire?

The walk continued southward, passing by the Supreme Court of Canada. The court is the final arbiter of justice in the country, ensuring that laws are applied fairly. The building itself, with its imposing architecture, is a testament to the importance of the rule of law, the corner stone was laid in 1939.

Turning eastward, the walk then meandered back towards the starting point with a slow pace. The sun, a soft caress on my skin, added warmth to the urban landscape, finally concluding around 4 PM.

April 11, 2025

The walk commenced around 10:00 AM and concluded approximately at 10:30 AM. The weather was partly cloudy.

Starting near the Rideau Canal, known for being the oldest continuously operated canal system in North America, the initial direction was generally southeast. The canal's waters reflected the pale blue of the sky.

Moving onward, the path approached Confederation Park. The park's floral displays were starting to show signs of spring, with various shades of green emerging. The park used to be a swamp and a railway yard.

Continuing, the route progressed closer to the National War Memorial. The bronze figures on the memorial are quite impressive. It was originally dedicated to Canadians who died in the First World War, but now represents all Canadian lives lost in conflicts.

Further along, the route led closer to the Ottawa River. The water had a grayish-blue hue. The Ottawa River is home to over 85 species of fish.

The pace quickened between the National War Memorial and the Ottawa River. Then slowed again, making a turn towards the north.

The concluding portion of the walk led back in a generally northwestern direction. Returning towards the start point, with the colors of Confederation Park becoming visible again.
